What is SHIMS-GH?

SHIMS-GH (School Health Information Management System for SCD) is a digital platform integrating student health profiles, daily monitoring, telemedicine, crisis reporting, and emergency preparedness tailored to SHS boarding schools. Its goal is to reduce the frequency and severity of sickle cell crises by enabling early detection, structured crisis management, and improved collaboration between students, staff, parents, and healthcare providers. fileciteturn0file0

Intervention design — 4 integrated modules

1. Digital Student Health Profiles

  • Diagnosis & genotype
  • Trigger & crisis history
  • Medication schedule
  • Emergency contacts & consent controls

2. Daily Monitoring & Early Warning

Students or nurses log pain, hydration, fatigue, temperature, exposure to cold, and infection symptoms. The system produces a risk status (high / moderate / low) to enable early action.

3. Emergency Preparedness

Night-time crisis protocol, emergency contact alerts, rapid parent/facility communication, and supply checklists (ORS, analgesics, warm packs).

4. Telemedicine Integration

Nurse-to-doctor teleconsultation, asynchronous messaging, and referral communication channels to ensure rapid clinical oversight.

Personalised (Individual)

  • Crisis monitoring dashboard
  • Personalised alerts for hydration, medication, rest
  • Student-specific risk scoring
  • Encrypted records & confidential telemedicine

Population (School / System)

  • School-wide emergency readiness tools
  • Trend dashboards for administrators
  • Training modules for staff
  • Anonymous reporting & aggregated heat maps

Key features

  • Early warning: automated risk scoring from daily logs
  • Crisis flow: structured night-time protocol & rapid alerts
  • Telemedicine: synchronous and asynchronous clinician access
  • Data privacy: consent-controlled visibility & encrypted records
  • Preparedness checklists: supply and procedure guidance
